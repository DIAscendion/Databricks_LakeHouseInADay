# Databricks Gold Layer Physical Data Model

## 1. Overview

This document defines the physical data model for the Gold layer in the Medallion architecture for the TMS (Transportation Management System) Shipment application. The Gold layer stores curated, aggregated, and dimensional data for advanced analytics, reporting, and business intelligence. It includes error and audit tables for robust data governance.

## 3. Data Retention Policies

### 3.1 Retention Periods for Gold Layer
- **Active Data**: 3 years from load_date
- **Error Data**: 5 years from first_occurrence_date
- **Audit Data**: 5 years from load_date
- **Aggregated Data**: 5 years from summary_date/summary_month

### 3.2 Archiving Strategies
- Data older than retention period is moved to a cold storage location (e.g., `/mnt/archive/gold/`)
- Use Databricks jobs to automate archival and deletion
- Maintain Delta Lake time travel for 30 days for rollback and recovery

## 6. Design Decisions and Assumptions

- All columns from Silver layer are retained in Gold layer for lineage and traceability.
- Added `id` fields (BIGINT) as surrogate keys for all Gold tables.
- Partitioned main fact table by `CREATED_DTTM` for query performance.
- Error and audit tables are partitioned by timestamp fields.
- No PK/FK constraints are enforced (Databricks/SparkSQL limitation).
- All tables use Delta Lake format for ACID compliance and time travel.
- Metadata columns (`load_date`, `update_date`, `source_system`) are included in all tables.
- Data retention and archiving are managed via Databricks jobs and Delta features.
- DDL scripts are compatible with Databricks Spark SQL.
- No unsupported constraints (PK/FK/UNIQUE/IDENTITY) are included.
